About DWAT-MENA 2026

Our mission, objectives, and host institution

Our Mission

MENA Drinking Water Conference

The MENA Drinking Water Conference (DWAT-MENA) is dedicated to fostering collaboration and knowledge exchange among water professionals, researchers, and policymakers across the MENA region. Our mission is to address the critical challenges facing drinking water resources — from tap to bottled and bulk water — and to promote sustainable, innovative solutions.

Objectives

Conference Objectives

  • Facilitate knowledge sharing on latest research and innovations in drinking water treatment and management
  • Promote collaboration among MENA countries to address shared water challenges
  • Discuss policy frameworks and regulatory approaches for sustainable water management
  • Showcase innovative technologies and best practices in water treatment and distribution
  • Address emerging contaminants and their impact on water quality and public health
  • Bridge the gap between scientific research and operational water management
Host Institution

CERTE

Water Research and Technologies Center (CERTE)

CERTE is a public research institution with approximately 90 permanent multidisciplinary researchers. Established in 2005 within the Technopark of Borj-Cédria, it operates under the supervision of the Tunisian Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research.

Its main mission is to conduct research, development, innovation, and technological development activities in the field of water, and to transfer scientific results to socio-economic sectors. CERTE is highly active at national, Mediterranean, and international levels across the entire water cycle.

Partner Laboratory

LaDVEN Laboratory

Created in 2006, the LaDVEN laboratory complements CERTE's expertise in the water field. It was established at the request of the Rural Engineering Directorate of the Ministry of Agriculture and SONEDE.

Invitation to Submit

Call for Papers – DWAT-MENA 2026

The MENA Drinking Water Conference (DWAT-MENA 2026) invites researchers, engineers, policymakers, and water industry professionals to submit original contributions related to drinking water quality, treatment, safety, and governance in the MENA region and beyond.

DW-MENA 2026 is dedicated to tap, bottled, and bulk waters. Contributions addressing fundamental research as well as applied and operational aspects are welcome. All submissions will undergo double-blind peer review by the Scientific Committee.

Submissions are invited for oral presentations and poster presentations. Selected authors may be invited to submit a full paper for publication in a peer-reviewed journal.

For Authors

Author Guidelines

Language

All submissions must be written in English.

Abstract Length

The abstract must be between 200 and 250 words (maximum 1 page).

Formatting Requirements
  • Font: Times New Roman
  • Abstract title: 14 pt, Bold
  • Section titles: 12 pt, Bold
  • Body text: 12 pt, Regular
  • Line spacing: 1.15
  • Margins: 2.5 cm on all sides
Required Abstract Structure
  • Title — concise and descriptive
  • Authors and affiliations — list all contributing authors
  • Keywords — 3 to 5 keywords
  • Background and objectives
  • Methodology and results
  • Conclusion
Originality

All submitted abstracts must represent original, unpublished work not currently under review elsewhere. By submitting, all listed authors confirm consent and originality of the work.

Presentation Formats

Authors may indicate their preference for oral or poster presentation. Final format is determined by the Scientific Committee. At least one author per accepted abstract must register for the conference.

How to Submit

Submission Guidelines

Where to Submit

All abstracts must be submitted electronically via the Microsoft CMT (Conference Management Toolkit) platform. The CMT submission link will be made available on this page once the conference site is created. You may also submit through the online submission form on this website.

How to Submit — Step by Step
  1. Create or log in to your Microsoft CMT account at cmt3.research.microsoft.com
  2. Navigate to the DWAT-MENA 2026 conference submission page
  3. Click "Create new submission" and select the appropriate topic area
  4. Fill in all required fields: title, authors, affiliations, abstract text, and keywords
  5. Indicate your preferred presentation format (oral or poster)
  6. Upload your abstract document if available (.doc, .docx, or .pdf, max 5 MB)
  7. Review your submission and click "Submit"
  8. You will receive an automatic confirmation email from CMT upon successful submission
Accepted File Formats

.doc · .docx · .pdf (maximum 5 MB)

Confirmation

Upon submission, you will receive an automatic acknowledgment email from the CMT system. If you do not receive a confirmation within 24 hours, please contact us at [email protected].
