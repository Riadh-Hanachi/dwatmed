Our Mission

The Drinking Water Mediterranean Congress (DWatMed) is dedicated to fostering collaboration and knowledge exchange among water professionals, researchers, and policymakers across the Mediterranean region. Our mission is to address the critical challenges facing drinking water resources and promote sustainable solutions.

Conference Objectives

  • Facilitate knowledge sharing on latest research and innovations in drinking water treatment and management
  • Promote collaboration among Mediterranean countries to address common water challenges
  • Discuss policy frameworks and regulatory approaches for sustainable water management
  • Showcase innovative technologies and best practices in water treatment and distribution
  • Address emerging contaminants and their impact on water quality and public health

Host Institution: CERTE

Water Research and Technologies Center (CERTE)

CERTE is a public research institution with approximately 90 permanent multidisciplinary researchers.

Established in 2005 within the Technopark of Borj-Cédria, it operates under the supervision of the Tunisian Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research. CERTE is a member of several national and international committees and application networks specialized in water-related solutions.

Its main mission is to conduct research, development, innovation, and technological development activities in the field of water, and to transfer scientific results to socio-economic sectors. CERTE is highly active at national, Mediterranean, and international levels across the entire water cycle (governance, management, RDI, evaluation), including the treatment and valorization of conventional and non-conventional waters, and adaptation to climate change.

LaDVEN Laboratory

Created in 2006, the LaDVEN laboratory complements CERTE's expertise in the water field. It was established at the request of the Rural Engineering Directorate of the Ministry of Agriculture and SONEDE. Its first action with these two organizations dates back to 1994, to prevent scaling of geothermal waters. Subsequently, several aspects related to the quality of natural waters and treatment or decontamination processes were developed.

Main research areas of LaDVEN:

  • Desalination and Water Valorization
  • Innovation in membrane processes and valorization of rejects
  • Water Quality and Monitoring
  • Water quality surveillance and analysis
  • Water Treatment and Depollution
  • Advanced treatment and water depollution
  • Sustainable Water Resources Management
  • Integrated management and water reuse
  • Geology and Hydrogeology
  • Study of groundwater resources and sedimentary basins
  • Advanced Materials for Environment
  • Nanomaterials and geopolymers for environmental applications

Terms and Conditions for Abstract Submission
1. Abstract Submission Guidelines

All abstracts must be submitted in English and follow the specified formatting guidelines. The abstract should be between 200-250 words and include the following sections: Introduction, Methodology, Results, and Conclusion.

2. Originality

Submitted abstracts must represent original work that has not been previously published or presented at other conferences. By submitting an abstract, you confirm that the work is original and that all authors have agreed to the submission.

3. Review Process

All abstracts will undergo a peer-review process by the Scientific Committee. The committee reserves the right to accept or reject abstracts based on relevance, quality, and adherence to submission guidelines. Notification of acceptance will be sent by January 10, 2026.

4. Presentation Format

Accepted abstracts may be scheduled for oral or poster presentation. The Scientific Committee will determine the presentation format based on the content and relevance to the conference themes. Presenters must register for the conference to confirm their participation.

5. Copyright

By submitting an abstract, authors grant DWatMed 2026 the right to publish the abstract in conference materials, including the program book, website, and other promotional materials. Authors retain copyright of their full work.

6. Registration Requirements

At least one author of each accepted abstract must register for the conference and pay the registration fee by the early registration deadline (January 15, 2026) to ensure inclusion in the program.

7. Withdrawal Policy

Abstracts may be withdrawn by notifying the conference organizers in writing before February 15, 2026. After this date, withdrawals may not be possible due to printing schedules.

8. Data Privacy

Personal information provided during abstract submission will be used solely for conference-related purposes and will not be shared with third parties without explicit consent.

9. Code of Conduct

All participants, including authors and presenters, are expected to adhere to professional standards of conduct and treat all participants with respect and courtesy.

10. Liability

The conference organizers are not liable for any loss, injury, or damage to persons or property during the conference. Presenters are responsible for their own travel arrangements and insurance.
